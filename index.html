About Me

I grew up as a kid who actually enjoyed vocabulary bees and reading. What started as memorizing definitions evolved into a deep love for language itself and how it works on a fundamental level. I've always been interested in how language works and how it can be applied to solve real-world problems, which led me to computational linguistics.

Along the way, I discovered competitive programming through USACO and found myself drawn to the puzzle-solving aspect of algorithms. As someone who had never loved math, learning the deeper aspects of programming beyond math opened my eyes to a new world. There's something satisfying about breaking down complex problems, whether it's optimizing a trading strategy or figuring out how to automatically detect Wikipedia vandalism.

These days, I spend my time tutoring other students in competitive programming, leading research in linguistics, and contributing to Wikipedia (with over 12,000 edits and counting). I'm particularly interested in how natural language processing and machine learning can solve real-world problems.

When I'm not coding or diving into linguistics, you'll find me working on projects that combine my interests. I'm also always ready to chat about a good book, new research, or politics.
