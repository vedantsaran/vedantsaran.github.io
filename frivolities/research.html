Flagging the Vandal: A Near-Real-Time Machine-Learning Pipeline for Wikipedia Vandalism Detection

June 2025 Research Paper

Abstract: Detecting vandalism early is critical to Wikipedia's knowledge integrity. We present a production-ready pipeline that combines a real-time revision stream, Transformer-based content analysis, and lightweight metadata features. Evaluating on the Wikidata Vandalism Corpus 2016 (82.3 M revisions, positive rate 0.0025%), our ensemble beats the current language-agnostic revert-risk model by +9 pp ROC-AUC and reduces median alert latency by 63%, while maintaining an on-line throughput of 4,800 revisions s‚Åª¬π on a single A10 GPU.

Key Findings

  • ROC-AUC: 0.956 (vs. 0.905 for current state-of-the-art)
  • Latency: 8.1ms median (vs. 22.1ms for API-based approach)
  • Throughput: 4,800 revisions/second on single A10 GPU
  • Dataset: 82.3M revisions from Wikidata Vandalism Corpus 2016

Methodology

  • Content Analysis: Fine-tuned BERT model for text classification
  • Metadata Features: 72 contextual features (user tenure, session entropy, etc.)
  • Ensemble: Weighted geometric mean of BERT probability and XGBoost score
  • Real-time Pipeline: EventStreams integration with sub-350ms alert latency
